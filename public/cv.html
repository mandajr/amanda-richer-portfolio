Displacement consultant, writer, and human-rights advocate working at the intersection of homelessness policy, community health, and the One Health framework, where human, animal, and environmental wellbeing are treated as inseparable. Her direct knowledge of homelessness runs through the work rather than beside it: guiding researchers working with displaced populations, advising public utilities and NGOs, securing resources for unhoused communities, and representing civil society from King County to the United Nations. She advocates for and helps shape policy addressing the root causes of homelessness, and carries it across writing, documentary photography, teaching, and public testimony.
